Cheapest Cash Pickups compared

When sending money overseas for cash pickup or to mobile wallets, **Ria Money Transfer** and **MoneyGram** are two of the largest providers globally. While services like Wise excel at bank deposits, Ria and MoneyGram dominate the cash payout market. Let's compare their fees, coverage, and speeds.

1. Agent Location Networks

**MoneyGram** has over 350,000 locations worldwide, with massive coverage across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. **Ria** has a slightly larger network with over 500,000 agent locations, making it highly accessible in retail environments, especially across South/Central America and parts of Europe.

Feature Ria MoneyGram
Locations 500,000+ 350,000+
Average Markup ~1.2% to 1.5% ~1.5% to 2.2%
Delivery Options Bank Deposit, Cash Pickup, Home Delivery Bank Deposit, Cash Pickup, Mobile Wallet
Online Platform Web and App (Ria app) Web and App (MoneyGram Online)

2. Exchange Rates and Markups

Both providers charge an exchange rate margin (markup) above the mid-market rate, but **Ria** generally offers slightly cheaper exchange rates for cash pickups. MoneyGram's margins tend to run slightly higher, especially for credit card funded instant cash pickups.

3. Mobile Wallet Payouts

If you are sending to mobile wallets like GCash, bKash, or M-Pesa, **MoneyGram** has stronger integrations, enabling direct instant sends to digital wallets in many emerging economies. Ria has been expanding wallet support, but MoneyGram has a slight edge in setup simplicity for digital receivers.

Summary Verdict

If you are looking for the absolute **cheapest fees for cash pickup**, **Ria** is usually the winner. However, if your recipient prefers **mobile wallet transfer** or you need to send to a remote area, check **MoneyGram's** coverage as their network might be more accessible.
