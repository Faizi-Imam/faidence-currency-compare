Which Is Cheaper in 2026?

When sending money internationally, two of the most popular platforms are **Wise** (formerly TransferWise) and **PayPal**. However, while both make it easy to move funds across borders, their underlying fee structures, exchange rates, and overall costs could not be more different.

1. Exchange Rate Comparison (The Hidden Markup)

This is where the biggest price difference lies. **PayPal** does not use the mid-market exchange rate. Instead, they add an exchange rate markup (usually **3% to 4%**) on top of the wholesale currency rate. This is a hidden fee that they pocket.

In contrast, **Wise** always uses the real mid-market exchange rate (the exact rate you see on Google or XE) and charges a transparent, upfront fee.

2. Upfront Transfer Fees

PayPal charges a flat fee of **$4.99** for international transfers funded via bank account or PayPal balance. If you fund using a credit/debit card, it scales up significantly. Wise uses a percentage-based fee that dynamically updates based on the amount. While Wise's upfront fee might occasionally look higher on very large amounts, their **zero exchange rate markup** makes them significantly cheaper overall.

3. Speed and Convenience

PayPal is the winner when it comes to speed. Transfers from PayPal account to PayPal account are instant. However, withdrawing that money to a local bank account can take 1-3 days. Wise transfers are executed via bank rails and usually take 1 to 2 business days, although some popular corridors are now instant.

Summary Verdict

If you want the **cheapest rates** and want to maximize the amount of money arriving in your recipient's bank account, **Wise is the clear winner**. PayPal is only recommended if you need the speed of a wallet-to-wallet transfer and both sender and recipient already have active PayPal accounts.
